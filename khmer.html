Grammar Rules
Afirmative Subject + verb + object + …
To be + noun Subject + jia + noun
Negative sentence Subject + ɔt/mən + verb + … + dtee
Noun + adjective If you need to use an adjective, make sure the noun goes before the adjective
Verb + adjective The verb 'to be' is dropped when used with an adjective
Asking a question with a question word To make a question, put the question word as the end of the sentence (except 'why' and 'who', which function as a subject)
Footnotes
*[nek] Nek is a written representation of the word 'you' without regard for age or status. Wherever you see [nek], it should be replaced with one of the following: bɔɔng, own, or the person's name (see subject pronouns section for more information)
1 Goes at the end of a sentence
2 When where is used with go or come: naa. When used with a word that already has the sound 'nəu̶': naa. Any other time: nəu̶-naa. Goes at the end of a sentence
3 Goes at the beginning of a sentence
